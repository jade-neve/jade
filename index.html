Bathroom Tips

  • Collect your shower, bath and basin water and re-use it to flush your toilet, water the garden and wash your car.
  • Only flush the toilet when necessary. Don’t use it as a dustbin.
  • Use a cup instead of running taps in the bathroom or kitchen when brushing teeth, shaving, drinking, etc.
Close

Indoor Tips

  • Use municipal drinking water only for essential washing, cooking and drinking purposes, and only use indoors.
  • Prevent taps from leaking by turning taps off lightly and replace washers as soon as they begin to leak.
  • Cut your water use to less than 50 litres per person, per day.
Close

Kitchen Tips

  • Defrost foods in the fridge or naturally, rather than placing it under running water.
  • Rinse glasses, cutlery and vegetables in a basin of water, reuse this water for potplants or in the garden.
  • Automatic dishwashers use up to 40 lt of water per load. Use a dishwasher with at least a 3 star rating, you can get this figure down to 18 lt per load and still have sparkling clean dishes.
Close

Laundry Tips

  • Wait for a full load before running washing machines and dishwashers. Re-use the water for the next wash.
  • Most washing machines have load adjustment buttons, set this to match the amount of washing you’re doing. If your machine does not have, wait for a full load.
Close

Lawn Tips

  • Plant indigenous or waterwise plants. Indigenous plants are genetically accustomed to the climate of South Africa. Exotic plant species are not accustomed to the climate and therefore need more watering.
  • Re-use your bath and sink water to water lawns. Professional greywater recycling systems are also great to save water.
Close

Outdoor Tips

  • Continue with pressure reduction programmes to reduce the flow of water at a time, as well as water losses through leakage in the pipework of the distribution system.
  • Install a grey-water system if feasible. They can save up to 1,500 litres of water per week.
  • Start collecting rain water. Water tanks can supply you with much needed rain that you can use whenever you want.
